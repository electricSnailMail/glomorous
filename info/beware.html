The person who designed glomorous is not Satoshi Nakamoto, the enigmatic genius behind the first blockchain database whose true identity remains shrouded in mystery. Though that would be really funny.

The person who designed glomorous is actually an amateur programmer who often learns things by making mistakes until they eventually lead to something that looks pretty.

glom noms and faves are stored in the browser if you close it and open it again. But the most reliable way to save your work is to use the copy button along the bottom. You can then paste everything into a file on your computer. The glom noms are copied in a specific format, so if you paste them into a .txt file, you can reupload them later using the upload button.
